  • What is Individual Disability Insurance?

    Individual Disability Insurance is a Long Term Disability policy that you own. It supplements other disability coverage to replace more of your income if you’re ever too hurt or sick to work for an extended period of time. The policy can’t be cancelled due to health conditions, and you can keep it if you change or leave your job, as long as you continue to pay your premiums.

  • How does Individual Disability Insurance protect my income?

    If you’re disabled, this coverage will pay you a monthly benefit designed to replace a percentage of your income while you can’t work. Added to other Long Term Disability coverage, IDI replaces more of your paycheck. That’s because it covers a higher percentage of your income, up to a higher maximum amount, and counts bonuses, commissions and other payments as income, not just your base salary.

  • What does Individual Disability Insurance cover?

    IDI pays you a benefit if an illness or injury keeps you from working and reduces your income. Certain conditions will apply, depending on the plan your employer has chosen.
